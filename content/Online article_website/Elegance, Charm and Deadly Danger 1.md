### The La's and more, 1986

The La’s were one of my favourite local bands during the mid 1980s. But most people who remember them from There She Goes won’t know that they were quite different when they started out.

They had two singers: Mike Badger, who originally formed the band, and Lee Mavers. I loved the fact that they were so different visually and musically – Mike (dark hair) with his 50s influences, Lee (blond) with his 60s style. That sort of creative tension is great for music but not so good for people, and Mike ended up leaving. (You can read about it in his book [The Rhythm and the Tide](https://www.liverpooluniversitypress.co.uk/doi/book/10.3828/9781781382585), published in 2015.)

Before that happened, they contributed a track to a compilation LP of bands from St Helens titled Elegance, Charm and Deadly Danger. St Helens is a town about ten miles from Liverpool that’s famous for its glass factory. During this period it had a thriving music scene, captured in the album – which was launched at the gig reviewed below.

I’ve just discovered video of the La’s doing the song from the LP, My Girl Sits Like A Reindeer (scroll down to see it). But as you can tell from the review, they weren’t the only good band involved, even if they are now the most famous.

## OLD MA CUXSOM AND THE SOAPCHOPPERS/WAKE UP AFRIKA! ROMULAN CLOAKING DEVICE/LAS/THE HAPPY GAULS/THE HOWITZER BROTHERS/MAVE

The System, Liverpool

MELODY MAKER, May 10, 1986

ST HELENS, for so long the idiot cousin of Liverpool, has finally got itself organised and shaped a musical community that’s as independent in spirit as in identity. There’s already an LP available called “Elegance, Charm And Deadly Danger” and now the live version follows. Six representatives – seven including their own poet/compere Mave – appear tonight.

First on are the Howitzer Brothers, inelegant, charming and slightly dangerous. They comprise two overcoats, a guitar, and a sense of responsibility for ensuring that we get a real alternative night out. Songs, words, a mimed poem, an existential blues number, a real poem, and something called “Real Man” which is part rant, part theatre, and part satire. A complete alternative cabaret in one set, if not in one number.

Next, charm. The Happy Gauls style themselves as Merseyside’s first surfing band and come complete with appropriate shirts. The Beach Boys were never like this, though. Their cheerfully aggressive sound is closer to the Ramones, and as bouncy as the singer’s ankles. I shall not forget the sight of the audience skipping round the group’s surfboard to an infectious affectionate reconstruction of “Living Next Door to Alice”.

Elegance: straighten your face for the Las who sing “My girl sits like a reindeer” without smiling. Their deadpan blues/rock’n’roll songs are slightly surreal and exquisitely cool.

I don’t think it’s exactly cool to call yourself something like Romulan Cloaking Device, but it does make smiling inevitable. They have as much wit and invention as you’d expect from the name and their singer has the presence to match.

And there’s more! Wake Up Afrika! are one of the busiest and most established of the company and prove to sound the most professional. This means that what they lose in danger they make up for in elegance. There are sounds that wash over you, but more often they stop and scrub, and that’s much more effective.

Equally bracing, to round off the night, come Old Ma Cuxsom and the Soapchoppers with manically sub-psychedelic keyboards and song titles like “Don’t cry darling, Daddy had to drown the cat”. Elegantly charming and dangerously deadly...

And fun. Seven acts in one evening – and I wasn’t bored once.

## Back in the 21st century

A new magazine about St Helens culture called Glass Eye launched this year. Only available in print locally but you can see some info [on their Instagram](https://www.instagram.com/glasseyemag/).

[Old Ma Cuxsom](https://www.instagram.com/old_ma_cuxsom/) got back together this year.

[Mike Badger](https://www.9x9records.co.uk/product-page/mike-badger-devils-ghosts-and-firearms-vinyl-lp) has a new LP out.
