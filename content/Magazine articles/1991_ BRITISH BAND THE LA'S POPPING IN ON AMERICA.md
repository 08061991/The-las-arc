description: Abstract- Add a bit of The Monkees and some Beatles and Stones, and you have The La's recently released self-titled debut, a "boss" album that's grabbed quite a bit of critical acclaim. Matter of fact, The La's summer-perfect single, 'There She Goes,' was recently praised by no less a pop authority than one-time dB and current R.E.M. sideman Peter Holsapple. The La's (short for lads) came together when [John Power] met lead vocalist and guitarist Lee Mavers in 1986. "I had dropped out of school at 16 and was just hanging out." Along with another lad named Mike Badger, who named the group but quit after a short time, the early La's were barely old enough to play pubs. "You have to be 18," noted Power. "We did maybe one cover song, `Jumpin' Jack Flash.' The rest were Lee originals.
